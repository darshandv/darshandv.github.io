This space is called what it is because that's exactly what it holds: thinking,
published before it's finished.

The usual instinct is to wait — flesh the idea out, structure it, make it
defensible, then publish. I think that instinct is quietly expensive, and
getting more expensive. Polish is cheap now; AI can polish anything. What
can't be generated is the baseline: the original observation, written down
while it's still alive. An unfinished thought published today can become a
bet, a system, or an essay two years from now. A thought that waited for
polish usually becomes nothing.

So, the rules here:

1. **The baseline is mine.** AI may help shape a sentence — it will never
   supply the thought.
2. **Small is legitimate.** Three sentences about garbage-collector pause
   times count. So does a half-formed idea about where agents are going, or
   something true about life that week.
3. **Unfinished is not careless.** Everything here is honestly thought, just
   not fully grown. Some entries will be revisited and contradicted later.
   That's the record working.

If you're reading this, you're on the inner track — the thinking before it
becomes the work. Start anywhere. There is no order.
