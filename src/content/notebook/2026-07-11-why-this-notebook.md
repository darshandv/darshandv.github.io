This is not a blog. Blogs come with a schedule, an audience to serve, and the
quiet guilt of the gap since the last post. This is a notebook that happens to
be public.

The rules I'm setting for myself:

1. **Anything qualifies.** A three-sentence observation about G1GC pause times
   is as legitimate here as a four-thousand-word teardown of a camera pipeline.
   Fragments are entries. Rabbit holes are entries. Life is an entry.
2. **Unpolished on purpose.** If I wait until an idea is defensible, I'll write
   four things a year and think none of them. The notebook optimizes for
   thinking, not for looking finished.
3. **Public because it sharpens.** Writing for a possible reader forces the
   half-thought to become a thought. That's the whole trade.

Why bother, when the work record already speaks? Because the record shows what
I shipped, not how I think. And the things I want to do next — the bets — will
come out of the thinking, not the résumé.

If you're reading this: welcome. Start anywhere. It's a notebook — there is no
order.
